Hey,

 

Sorry for being so late with this invitation. Anyway, this coming Saturday,
Dec. 5 from 2p-4p, I have booked a meeting room at Ruiz Branch Library for
people who want to perform music for a supportive audience. 

 

This should be a good opportunity to do a "dress rehearsal" for a recital or
a future open mic performance. Or you can attend and listen. 

 

You don't have to play ukulele to participate--other acoustic instruments
are welcome.

 

We will have a signup sheet for performers so you may want to get here at 2p
(being fashionably late may cause you to play after Mr Shimabukuro or Sungha
Jung <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x3uZAAjqDsc> ).

 

Since we have two hours, you may be able to perform your song more than
once. Or play other songs.

 

Feel free to invite friends but the capacity is ~35 people so please be
careful about mass invitations. 

 

If this event works out, we may want to have monthly or quarterly events
like this.

 

Ruiz Branch Library

http://library.austintexas.gov/ruiz-branch

1600 Grove Blvd., Austin, TX, 78741

 

FWIW: Library meeting room rules

http://library.austintexas.gov/meeting-rooms/policies 

If you bring food or drink please read the library meeting room policies. 

No amplified music...acoustic is ok

No alcoholic beverages (duh)

No gambling :)

 

I hope you can attend.

 

Regards,
