Folk Uke at the end of the first episode.

The song is just three chords and has easy strumming so it’s perfect for
beginners.
Suggested Strumming

You can use the old faithful strum almost all the way through:

d – d u – u d –

In the chorus and the solo: Play that pattern twice for the C chords at the
end of the first and third lines. Play it once for everything else. So it
sounds like this:

http://ukulelehunt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/MFGFUSTrum.mp3
<http://ukulelehunt.us6.list-manage.com/track/click?u=1eeebafea377cdfdf02b5d41c&id=a238ea257e&e=5b4983835b>
Strum
<http://ukulelehunt.us6.list-manage.com/track/click?u=1eeebafea377cdfdf02b5d41c&id=f5b85b82a2&e=5b4983835b>

The only place that strum doesn’t work is in the outro. There switch to
just down-strums. One for the G at the end of the line and two for
everything else. For the very last line go back to the main strum for two
chords then end on a down-strum on the C.
