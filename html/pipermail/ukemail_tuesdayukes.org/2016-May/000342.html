> Hi, gang. Here's the gear we were talking about tonight!
> 
> Roland AC-33 amp
> This is a small acoustic amp that a lot of people really like with ukuleles. At 30 watts, it's not very powerful, but it has a built-in looper and can run on batteries. It's pricey at over $400, so there are better deals out there. There are also 40W and 60W versions for a similar price, without the battery option.
> I bought my AC-33 used.
> http://www.sweetwater.com/store/detail/AC33RW
> 
> Fishman Loudbox Mini amp
> The Mini is another popular acoustic amp for ukuleles. It's got 60 watts, much more powerful than the Roland, and is probably more rugged. Sweetwater has the best deal, offering a package with a free Sennheiser microphone, mic stand and mic cable. $330.
> Brock and Charlie both have this package.
> http://www.sweetwater.com/store/detail/LoudBoxMiniPK
> 
> LR Baggs Para Acoustic D.I.
> This preamp with EQ really improves the sound and volume of a passive pickup. At $189, it's jumped in price since I bought mine, but you can often find them on Craigslist for about $100. Takes a 9V battery.
> http://www.sweetwater.com/store/detail/ParaDI
> 
> Pickups
> This blog post has some helpful info on pickups. 
> My Pono has a passive under-saddle pickup and sometimes sounds kind of tinny or brittle plugged in, but the D.I. warms up the sound a lot. 
> I hear good things about the K&K Twinspot, also a passive pickup, and the MiSi, an active pickup (active pickups have built-in preamps, so most need a battery, but the MiSi is rechargeable/batteryless).
> http://liveukulele.com/gear/amplifying-your-ukulele/pickups/
