I've mentioned this to folks in our meetings - but for anyone who missed that - we're (loosely) organizing a group of folks to do singalongs for folks in assisted living, alzheimer care, nursing care, etc - we're calling the group Senior Sing. We'll have standard song books  - one for now - and each singalong will be about a dozen songs. You can play the uke and sing, just play the uke, just sing, play a kazoo, play the shakers, play a ZED chord, fake the whole thing - the more folks we have at a singalong - the more participation we get from the audience  - and the key to this is participation. 

Our first rehearsal will be Sunday, January 12 - 12:30-2:30 at the Brewtorium.  We'll rehearse once a month - though if we're playing a lot we may knock that down to "when we need to rehearse".

We'll have a mailing list - which is how we'll annouce most of our events. We'll also list them on the TUG web site calendar - just type "Senior Sing" in the search bar and you should see all the upcoming events. 

If you're interested, you don't need to come to every event, any level of participation will be welcome. 

We've got a summary of Senior Sing on the web site. 

We'll have mailing list  sign up sheets at our weekly meeting - and you can also just reply to this email and say - sign me up - if you'd like to get  on the mailing list. 

Thanks!
