Not too long after I first started playing, I took a couple of lessons via
Skype with Rob MacKillop (if you're not familiar with him, check out his
websites, he's an amazing instrumentalist).  At the time, I had four of his
uke books already.  In our second lesson, he asked me to play a piece he
composed, *Tears for Amalia*, that is included in his "20 Easy Fingerstyle
Studies for Ukulele" songbook.  (Word to the wise, some are not that easy,
haha.)  Anyway, I played it for him and was pretty relieved because I
didn't make any egregious errors; you know, while playing I was ready for
it to go into the ditch at any minute.  But, managed to make it through
okay.  Rob then told me, "You played the correct notes in the correct
order, but you left all of the music out of it."   The point being, I think
that is what Daniel means in regard to going deeply into a music piece in
order to get more out of it.  You can't just play the correct notes in the
correct order (though, that certainly beats playing incorrect notes and/or
in the incorrect order!).  Now, if I can just learn how to do that... ;-)

Daniel's *Arpeggio Meditations* books is just awesome, can't recommend it
enough for anyone who wants to improve their fingerstyle skills.
