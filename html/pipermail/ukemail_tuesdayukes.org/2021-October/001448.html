Christine,

As Vany wrote, 0232 is Cdim/G, but in the context of your song, that
mystery chord seems to me like it's trying to be G#7 (because in the song,
you always slide down to G7 immediately after the G#7). G#7 would be 1323,
not 0323, or you could only strum three of the strings: x323.

I also think you could fairly name 0232 as G#7/G. I kind of expect a G#7/G
to have too much dissonance between the G and G#, but the ukulele chord
0323 does not actually contain a G# (the notes are G, D#, F#, C), so the
dissonance is implied without being overpowering. There is still the
dissonance between the G and F#, but on my low-G uke, that is nearly an
octave apart. I'd expect that dissonance to be more in-your-face on a
high-G uke. Since this chord always appears during the cat's ah-wooing, a
little intentional dissonance seems to fit the song really well.

By the way, here's the cat song I mentioned on Tuesday (
https://youtu.be/r4OhIU-PmB8). Anybody who recently acquired a bass uke
might want to watch this play-along version (https://youtu.be/h0Ckq7qRwSg).

Paul
