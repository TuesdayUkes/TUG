I've got 3 ensemble pieces for us to look at for this spring. 

The Liliting Banshee is a traditional Irish Jig - it's in 6/8 time, and using campanella fingering so it works nicely with high-g ukes. There is a melody part, an arpeggiated chord part, and a bass/baritone part. 

In Heaven There Is No Beer is a polka. There is a melody part and 2 harmony parts, along with chords and lyrics in English and German. 

Yaka Hickey Hula Dula is a fake Hawaiian tune from 1915. There are 3 parts plus chords and lyrics. 

I've attached MP3s of the arrangements here so you can listen to them - along with a link to the Dropbox where PDFs for all the parts reside. I'll bring them on my iPad to the next meeting - along with some printed copies and some notebooks for folks who don't have them. Once we figure out what parts we each want to work on I can bring printouts as well. 

Here's a link to the Dropbox -
