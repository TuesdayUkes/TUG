The lady at Highland Elementary is excited about TUG coming to play Hawaiian songs for their international day celebration. We probably need 2-3 more besides Ulapalakua. My wife - Karen - is going to be taking a Hula class - and she's going to see if she can recruit a couple  more folks to dance. 

A meeting or two ago folks seemed interested in doing a polka piece - in that vein - here is a 4-part ensemble arrangement I put together for Aloha Oe - maybe we could do this as an instrumental break when we sing that song. I'm also attaching the words and chords - and an MP3 of the score so you can get an idea of what it would sound like. The baritone part has chord shapes for regular uke - so we could use a regular uke there if we needed to. Each part is here with notation and TABS - and TABS only (which is less busy if you don't read notation).  I'll also bring print outs of the parts to the next TUG and we can take a look at it. 

(Oh - I'm also attaching our version of Uklapalkua - so if you don't have that - here it is)

See you Tuesday!
