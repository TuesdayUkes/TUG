Here's a version of Kerr's Negro Jig I put together. Negro Jigs were pre-civil war banjo tunes played by African Americans. The minstrel banjo (as it was called) of the time was a gut stringed instrument - which was played almost exclusively by African Americans - which had a sound not all that different from our ukuleles. This version is from the Oxford Slow sessions - it's a neat little jig in D minor. 

Tenor Uke - high melody
Baritone Uke - low melody
Banjo Uke - rhythm
U Bass - Bass

https://soundcloud.com/kevin-jolly-438109743/kerrs-negro-jig

Hear the Rhiannon Giddens and the Carolina Chocolate Drops do it the right way -

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G3-xl6MpZus

It's interesting how low these early banjos are
