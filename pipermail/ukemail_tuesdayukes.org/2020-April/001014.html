On Wed, Apr 29, 2020 at 11:09 AM Kevin Jolly <kevin at oldtexas20.com> wrote:

> Here's a version of Kerr's Negro Jig I put together. Negro Jigs were
> pre-civil war banjo tunes played by African Americans. The minstrel banjo
> (as it was called) of the time was a gut stringed instrument - which was
> played almost exclusively by African Americans - which had a sound not all
> that different from our ukuleles. This version is from the Oxford Slow
> sessions - it's a neat little jig in D minor.
>
> Tenor Uke - high melody
> Baritone Uke - low melody
> Banjo Uke - rhythm
> U Bass - Bass
>
